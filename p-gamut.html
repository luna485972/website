Gamut

Borne out of a 5-days-workshop led by Jeremy Leslie, this magazine is a visual and conceptual exploration of the way touch— or its absence—shapes the way we connect. Through the various issues of our magazines we wanted to explore the different modes of embodied relationships that are created through the presence or absence of physical touch.

The scope of our connections in this sense refers to both the aim that our relationships have and the physical field in which they take place. The first issue, Greetings, explores how the rituals that surround the exchange of greetings take shape across different cultures, contexts, and subcultures—and how they can express distance or closeness, both physically and emotionally.

Using 4 different papers, Gamut takes into account the materiality of the magazine, and uses it to create a new layer of meaning. The ephemerality of the Sixties is used for the Photo Gallery remarks the ephemerality of memories captured by this photographic project about the topic of leaving; while the poster's MyStardream Red paper, goes along with the boldness of the content itself, showing and index of gang greeting signs.

The binding consists in staples and rubber bands in three different colours reminding the RGB gamut and the range of distance and closeness between extremes, symbolised by the stretching of the rubber bands themselves.
