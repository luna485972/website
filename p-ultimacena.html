Cena

The brief asked us to begin with an in-depth analysis of a director's filmography of our choosing, then use it as the foundation for four connected deliverables: a short film, a visual identity for a fictional production company, a promotional campaign, and a behind-the-scenes feature.

We chose Martin Scorsese. His filmography revealed two distinct narrative threads: gritty gangster sagas and psychological thrillers. We gravitated toward the latter, anchoring our project in Shutter Island.

Through extensive research and brainstorming, we shaped a script and storyboard drawing on Scorsese's rich visual language, built around three core themes: obsession, self-destruction, and loss of faith.

The film opens with an animated title sequence inspired by Saul Bass, pulling the viewer straight into its unsettling atmosphere. Sickly yellows and clinical blues mark the protagonist's gradual descent into madness.

That same visual sensibility carries into the brand identity, where a red REC circle becomes a recurring graphic motif, and heavily textured illustration and typography echo the tone of the story throughout. Finally, the promotional campaign and behind-the-scenes feature round out the project. The latter blends references to iconic Scorsese shots with a comedic, narrative-driven framing device, letting us document our process with both authenticity and humor.
