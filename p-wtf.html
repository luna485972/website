What
To Fix

What To Fix was born with the intention of pointing out how and why mainstream ecology is currently failing. As we change perspective and start to analyze structural implications of the ecological problem, it becomes strikingly clear that ecology cannot fulfill its cause without being intersectional. Whenever the environment is neglected, human rights follow through, and those in already precarious situations pay the highest price. The opposite is also true: the violation of human rights leads to a violation of the environment.

Based on these insights the magazine looks at different corners of the world, amplifying the voices that go unheard to unveil the thread of injustice that strangles us and the planet. From Cambodian Sopheap daily sifting through mounds of garment waste as a result of carbon colonialism to Rome planting greenery to displace homeless people in the name of sustainability, we explore environmental rights and wrongs to redefine ecology through a new lens.

WTF's first issue is the Earth Issue, and it's divided in 3 different sections: Underground (making “invisible” people visible), Bad Seed (giving a voice to the outcasts of society), and Unroot (peoples forced to leave their land). Visually, they are differentiated with colors and the quotes' treatment.

While the topics discussed are serious, playful puns and illustrations are the main communicative thread of the magazines, so that we could catch the interest of all people, including those that are exhausted by the topic of climate change.

The transmediality of design is explored with the creation of a board game, Write The Future, that teaches ideas about collaboration, altruism, care through the simple act of play.
